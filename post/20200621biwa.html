Biwa Lake
Environmental photography 2020-06-21

According to Encyclopædia Britannica, Lake Biwa located in center of Shiga Prefecture of Japan is the largest Japanese freshwater lake.　 The water surface covers 670.3 km² and the circumference is 241 km. It is said that the name comes from the shape of the lake which resembles a biwa, a Japanese string instrument.

It was March. The cherry flowers were already in bloom in Otsu located on the south tip of Lake Biwa. From Otsu the reeds grew tall along the East side of the lake. In the surroundings mosquitoes already swarmed, eagles gazed from utility poles, and from time to time water fowl scattered across the sky.







Around 18:00 the sun descended behind Mount Hiei. The light from the streetlamps on Lake Biwa Bridge, which spans between Moriyama and Otsu, turned on. Besides the reflection the water surface fell into the dark.
