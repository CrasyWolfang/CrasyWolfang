One step into Nature
Environmental photography 2020-08-28

If one was to break off from one of Kansai's mountain roads they would quickly find themselves in the forest. There they would most likely understand why there are people who hate nature. There are a lot of mosquitoes, and from time to time they will notice that some strange bug have climbed up their arm. However, if they get used to the insects and give their time, they will come to feel the tranquility of nature. The sky slowly moves above. The creeks flow reliably. The forest is peaceful.

Life prospered along the creek. Various animals, ranging from tiny ants to one-and-a-half meter long snakes, coexisted in balance. Even though, depending on where and when you looked, the animals were be different it felt as if they all were linked to the flow of water. There were crabs in the creek that climbed and walk on around on the rocks. Small frogs jumped up from the creek, and above flew dragonflies in circles. With a resemblance to a rope being pulled, a snake came crawling for prey. Different insects made an appearance, and wherever you looked you would find ants.

Life also flourished on the river that the creek flowed into. In the water were fish. Also here were dragonflies in the airspace above the surface.









Unfortunately even on the mountain human traces could be found. Even far into the forest was plastic left behind, and cheers from the neighboring town echoed through the valley.

There were a few broken down motorcycles parked at the entrance of the forest on a crumbling trail. Along the main river was a road built for cars, and across the river was dilapidated houses. The paths around the houses seemed neglected. There were fallen over trees in the path, thrown trash on the river bank, and next to the path was a dry skeleton from a deer.


The forest was never quiet. The creek trickled with water, cicadas sang from morning to evening, and in the middle of the night birds screamed loudly from treetop to treetop. When night came the star sky rolled in above the trees. And, if you would turn on a flashlight there would be a frog or a cricket staring back at you.

This was one step into nature. The bears and raccoon dogs that live in the area can adapt to changes. However, if the creek would run dry or the forest would be lodged, it would certainly completely change the life for the animals in the vicinity.
