A Beaten Road
Environmental photography 2021-06-01

Month of May. It was a sunny day in Kyoto. Boats were parked on land besides the river by Kameoka Station. Those boats belong to the Hozu Boat River Ride. Flatbed boats that depart from Kameoka, slither their way through the mountain valley and eventually float to Saga. In the old days the water currents here were used for transportation of goods. Timber, grains, firewood and more would be transported from Tanba to Kyoto and Osaka on flatbed boats via the water.（www.hozugawakudari.jp (jp)） However, these days it appears that only tourists come to enjoy the river ride.

If one continues on the road from Kameoka Station across Oi River they'll eventually arrive at the seemingly popular Sakura Park. Sakura is the name for the Japanese cherry blossom and from the name one can already understand that the park is themed around these trees. A quick look at the park's website (jp) and you'll quickly learn that there are 280 cherry trees from 28 different species in the park. Next to the park is the Nanatani River and along the river is a walk path with cherry trees lined along the path. The walk path is called Yawaragino Michi which means peaceful road.




On a warm day the Nanatani river flows until the park where it runs dry. If one turns around and follows the water upriver they will quickly be out of Kameoka. From here there is nothing but the mountain road. There aren't any people on the road and nature unfolds on each side, so it's a climb to be enjoyed. The sound of birds chirping, water splashing around in a waterfall, the wind moving between branches and the trees slowly swaying, only interrupted by the eventual car that drives by.

An old man came walking from the direction of Mount Atago. On his stroll he met a photographer and they stroke up some small chat. In the scenery behind them the pedals from a cherry tree fluttered away with the wind. A skink crawled away from their shadows over some dry leaves. At the moment it seemed as if there were no one on the road besides the two.

After their small talk and some recommendation the two parted ways. The old man continued his walk towards Kameoka and the photographer continued his climb towards Mount Atago. Just 5 minutes away from the parting point was a small lake. It was from this silent lake that the Nanatani River flowed. Further down the road past the lake were fields for cultivation. Alongside the road was a small creek with salamanders calmly walking around, and on a branch wedged over the creek stood a kingfisher that took flight when it noticed a photographer walking up the road. From there it was just a small climb until arrival into one of Kyoto's villages.



From the village it was about another kilometer until the mountain pass. From there it was downhill. The road passed through the scenery of Hozu and eventually led to Arashiyama in Kyoto. Katsura River flowed here until Osaka and beyond and from time to time one could see one of the flatbed boats from Hozu Boat River Ride float by. From here the boats would be loaded onto trucks and driven back up to Kameoka and returned to their launch site. From there the cycle would start over again.
