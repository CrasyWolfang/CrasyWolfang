Soft Wind
Environmental photography 2020-08-23

If one where to take the Sanin Main Line from Kyoto Westwards they would pass into a tunnel past the famous Arashiyama. Once out of the tunnel they would arrive at Hozukyo station on top of a bridge. If they would get off there they would be likely to forget that the city lied just behind the hill. In the valley beneath the bridge a river flowed, a lizard crawled into a gap in the bridge, and as the scenery stood open the sun glowed hot. If the soft wind blew it gave of a refreshing relief from the heat.

The mountain road was quiet. There were very few people on the twisting roads. From place to place there were a few people relaxing along the river. Rock-climbers and cyclist could also be encountered.

Besides the road, the hills, the bridges, and the view over the valley, there were also some creeks gently flowing by the road. If one were to run up to a creek and dip their head into the water they would realize that they were not alone. There were crabs in the creek and insects and spiders liked the vicinity.







Once over the mountain pass was the shade of the forest. If one climbed down from there they would shortly arrive to Arashiyama and the atmosphere have a complete change. Instead of the mountain road was a city street. Instead of the creeks along the river were old-style restaurants. The area seemed as if it was for tourists.



There was a lot of temples in Arashiyama. In the middle was a pond, and, of course, there was the famous bamboo forest and a lot of tourists. If one climbed up into the park from there they would get one more view over the river, and shortly after the descend they would be back in the city.
