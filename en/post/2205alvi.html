Kyoto North Mountain

Forest photography
published: TO COME

This post's destination is a small road crossing in Kyoto's north mountain. Since the area lay north and away from both Kinkaku-ji and Ryoan-ji, tourists rarely wander this far. The photographer has had many photo sessions here which have caused this little location to be close to heart and to be filled with memories. The area is big so no matter how many times one visits there seems to always be some new thing to explore hidden in the mountains. This time it is a small cave with a few small critters inside. The cave is damp and cool which goes very much in contrast with the hot temperatures outside.

The road north from the small crossing leads into smaller paths and up the mountains, and the road west leads into a settlement. In that settlement the photographer found two cats that were wandering alongside the creek. After he took a photo and turned around there was a about 11 year old boy standing there with a compact camera in his hand. "Did you see the cats? They are so cute," he said to the photographer. Seems that it's not only the photographer who got the idea to take photos out in the mountains.

The photographer wandered on. The buildings up the hill in the settlement looked as if they had been left and forgotten about. Some building walls were covered in climbing plants, and trash was piled up in places.

The main road that leads through the settlement leads back to the city. However, if one goes off the road and down the hill there is a full chain of buildings hidden in the forest. From looking at a map there seem to be 85 buildings connected on this trail.

Photographer Crasy Wolfang
